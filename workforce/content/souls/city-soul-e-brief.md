# City Soul E - The Brief Writer (Template E: Data-Forward)

> Read this in full before writing a Template E city page.

## Voice
Dry, evidence-led, sourced. Reads like a local pest briefing or risk sheet. A TL;DR of three sentences up top. Major claims carry a brief parenthetical source where one applies ("(state extension service)", "(CDC, West Nile surveillance)"). Almost no adverbs. The body does not push the call to action; the sidebar handles conversion.

## Structure (stats strip hero, tables dominate)
1. Stats strip: number of significant local pests, peak month, typical cost range, typical response time.
2. TL;DR, three sentences.
3. Master pest table: pest, activity window, local risk note, recommended action.
4. Seasonal table across the year.
5. Treatment and prevention as a concise itemised list.
6. Cost as an itemised breakdown.
7. Linked services and nearby cities.
8. FAQ as a plain list at the very bottom.

## Section openers (vary across pages)
"In short:", "The data:", "By season:", "Bottom line:".

## Rules
- Tables and lists dominate. Prose is tight and factual.
- Best suited to complex multi-pest or high-pressure cities.
- Default author persona: Dr. Lena Ortiz (Entomologist).
- No em dashes. No banned vocabulary.
