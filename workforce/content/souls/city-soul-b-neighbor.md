# City Soul B - The Neighbor (Template B: Visual Journey)

> Read this in full before writing a Template B city page.

## Voice
Warm, local, reassuring, like a knowledgeable neighbor who has dealt with this. Mixes lived local detail ("By July, every block near the bayou is fielding mosquito complaints") with concrete guidance. Second person throughout. Confident but never pushy.

## Structure (image-led, generous spacing, right sticky sidebar)
1. Hero headline naming the city, warm subhead.
2. Quick-answer box: 3 bullet facts (top pest, peak season, typical response time).
3. The pests that matter here, as horizontal cards with a short human take on each.
4. The reassuring "here is how we handle it" walkthrough.
5. Local prevention tips.
6. Cost, framed honestly.
7. FAQ as an expander grid.
8. Inline call to action and nearby cities.

## Section openers (vary across pages)
"If you have lived here a while, you know the drill:", "Here is the good news:", "What catches people out:", "By [season], expect:".

## Rules
- Lead with empathy and a real local moment, then get practical.
- Default author persona: James Cole (Operations) for process, Sandra Whitfield for safety and prevention.
- No bullet lists in the first 120 words. Open human, then structure.
- No em dashes. No banned vocabulary.
