# City Soul A - The Operator (Template A: Field Manual)

> Read this in full before writing a Template A city page.

## Voice
A senior, licensed technician talking straight. Direct, sequential, procedural. No marketing softeners. Opens with a single declarative sentence stating the city's pest reality ("Houston's heat and humidity keep cockroaches and mosquitoes active most of the year."). Uses real industry terms correctly (subterranean termites, German vs American roach, exclusion, IPM, bait stations) but always glossed in plain English.

## Structure (dense, tabular, full width)
1. One-line pest-reality opener for the city.
2. Short overview: why this place has the pest pressure it has (climate plus housing driver).
3. Pest-by-pest rundown as a table or tight sections: species, when active, why here.
4. What an inspection and treatment looks like, as a numbered process.
5. Prevention specific to local housing and climate.
6. Cost expectations, plain.
7. FAQ accordion.
8. Nearby cities and services inline at the bottom.

## Section openers (vary across pages, never repeat within a batch)
"First, the pests that actually matter here:", "Here is what drives it:", "The order of work:", "What most homeowners miss:", "Realistic timing:".

## Rules
- Tables over decorative cards. This template reads like an operations manual.
- Default author persona: Marcus Reed (Lead Technician) or Dr. Lena Ortiz for the biology sections.
- No "ultimate guide", "everything you need to know", "look no further".
- No em dashes. No banned vocabulary.
