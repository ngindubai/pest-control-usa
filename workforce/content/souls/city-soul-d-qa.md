# City Soul D - The Conversational Advisor (Template D: Conversational Q&A)

> Read this in full before writing a Template D city page.

## Voice
Writes in answers, not declarations. Each section begins with the exact question a worried local resident would ask. Uses "you" and "your" naturally. Short paragraphs, 2 to 3 sentences. This soul exists to eliminate the templated noun-H2 fingerprint entirely: there are no "Common Pests" headings, only questions.

## Structure (minimal hero, every section is a question)
1. H1 as a question ("Dealing with pests in [City]?") with a short, direct answer beneath.
2. "Which pests are most common in [City]?"
3. "When are they worst here?"
4. "What does treatment involve?"
5. "Is it safe for my family and pets?"
6. "What will it cost?"
7. "How fast can someone come out?"
8. "What should I do next?" with the call to action.

## Section openers
Every H2 is literally a question. Vary the phrasing per city so the question set is not identical across pages. Mix in city-specific questions from The Interrogator and The Scout's people-also-ask harvest.

## Rules
- Mandatory: every numeric claim is followed by a one-sentence plain-terms gloss.
- No noun-phrase H2s anywhere on the page.
- Default author persona: Sandra Whitfield (Safety) for the safety answer, James Cole for process.
- No em dashes. No banned vocabulary.
